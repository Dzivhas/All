If you’re a technology nerd, enthusiast or your contract is about to expire and thinking about upgrading. You have sure heard that Samsung have recently( as they do every year) released the new S20, S20+ and S20 Ultra as their new Flagship devices. In this article we will be focussing on the S20 as it is the direct descendant of the previous line in almost every way.

Samsung did not fail to reach for the skies when the conjured the specs for this particular device. The Samsung S20 boost a significant improvement in all aspects including but not limited to screen, camera, processors and battery.

The S20 boosts a 6.2” Quad HD (3200*1440p) OLED panel that as usual will most likely be the best screen you can get on a smartphone for the next 12 months. Additionally the screen runs at a blistering 120 Hz enabling instantaneous response to your every touch no matter how slight. They further doubled the touch response so as to make the entire experience. However, the phone comes by default at 60 Hz running at 1440p resolution and can only reach the 120hz if set to 1080p. The screen is made from Gorilla glass 6 resulting in less susceptibility to scratches and drops.

The new flagship will be using the New Exynnos 990 processing core, which is said to be as fast as, or close to the Snapdragon 865. This mean the devices will as expected be very snappy in daily operations and mobile gaming. Although the 990 is based on architecture from the previous year (ARM Cortex A76) the difference would miniscule and unnoticed by many users. The processor will be adequately utilised as the S20 will have 8gb of ram and 128gb of internal space.

Camera have recently become one of if not the most important feature for many users. In this respect Samsung is not disappoint. There are 3 cameras in the back and 1 selfie camera through the infinity O display. The back has a 12mp main camera at f1.8, a 64mp telephoto lens at f2.0 and a 12mp wide lens at f2.2. All sensor are bigger from the previous year and definitely will improve low light photos. It further has 30x software zoom and 10x optical zoom with added zoom related improvement to the camera app. The selfie camera is a 10mp sensor at f/2.2The S20 is also capable of 8k video quality which is guaranteed to satisfy videographers and vloggers alike.Other improvement include the now larger battery at 4000mAh and a fast charger. With a flatter screen and no native Bixby button and under screen fingerprint sensor and again no headphone jack.

If one considers the 120hz and the improvements all across the board it will make this device a contender in the flagship market even though it will cost R18 999.00 to own.
